Snapchat

Since January 2015, Snapchatters have been using filter to transform themselves into puppies, flower princesses and babies. However, some of the filters are not only racist, but also sexist. When a beauty filter is applied, the user’s face morphs into a brighter, often light-skinned, and blemish-free of the orignal to suit Western standards of beauty. According to Joy Buolamwini’s research with the MIT Media Lab, facial recognition technology works significantly less for darker-skinned females. Compare to 0.8% of error rate for lighter-skinned male, there’s 34.4% increase for darker-skinned female.

Facebook

In 2016, a report from ProPublica showed that Facebook’s ethnic affinities demographic could filter racial groups from ad reach—a violation of federal laws. Its algorithm unknowingly helped spread discrimination. For example, Facebook’s algorithm may decide to aim an ad for high-end real estate at an affluent zip code, which demographically skews white, thus violating the Fair Housing Act.
